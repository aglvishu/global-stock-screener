Slide 213:  1.DSRI ( Days Receivable Index) = ( Net Receivable t / Sales t) /  ( Net Receivable t-1 / Sales t-1)  Net Receivable = Trade Receivable ; Sales = Net Operating Revenue   Coefficient : + 0.920  Inference: If Current year AR/Sales is higher than that of the previous year, then the firm is having pressure to  manipulate its financials 
Slide 214: 2. Gross Margin Index (GMI) = ( Sales t-1  - COGS t-1 / Sales t-1)  /  ( Sales t  - COGS t / Sales t)  Coefficient : + 0.528  Inference: If  previous year GPM is higher than that of the current year, then the firm is manipulating….when GPM comes down, firm has the pressure  to manipulate  
Slide 215: 3. Asset Quality Index (AQI) ( 1- { CA t +PPE t+ Securities t}/ TA t)                  /  ( 1- { CA t-1 +PPE t-1+ Securities t-1}/ TA t-1)  Coefficient : + 0.404  Inference: If Current year intangible assets to TA  ratio is higher than that of the previous year, then the firm is manipulating its financials   
Slide 216: What is the AQI for Marico in 2025 ? = ( 1- { CA t +PPE t+ Securities t}/ TA t)                  /  ( 1- { CA t-1 +PPE t-1+ Securities t-1}/ TA t-1)  PPE here = sum of Tangible assets & Capital WIP Securities = Non-current investments   
Slide 217: 4. Sales Growth Index ( SGI) = Sales t  /  Sales t-1 Coefficient : + 0.892  Inference: If Current year Sales is higher than that of the previous year, then the firm is manipulating its financials   
Slide 218: 5. Depreciation Index ( DEPI)  ( Dep t-1 / {PPE t-1 +Dep t-1})                      /  ( Dep t / {PPE t +Dep t}) Coefficient : + 0.115  Inference: If Current year figure is lower than that of the previous year, then the firm is manipulating its financials   
Slide 219: 6. SGA expense Index (  SGAI) = ( SGA expense t/ Sales t) / (SGA expense t-1/ Sales t-1) Coefficient : -0.172  Inference: If Current year SGA/Sales is lower than that of the previous year, then the firm is manipulating its financials

Slide 225: Benford’s Law ( or Law of Anomalous Numbers or The First Digit Law )    By Astronomer Simon Newcomb in 1881  Popularized by Physicist Frank Benford in 1938 Number One shall be the Leading Digit in a fair data set of numbers for 30.1% of the distribution; No 2 for 17.6% of the distribution; other numbers with decreasing frequency Log (1 + {1/n} ) E-g :  Log (1 + {1/1}) = Log (2) = 0.3010           Log (1+ {1/2}) = Log (1.5 ) = 0.1760   Earlier number have higher frequency of occurrence compared to later numbers. Which means No 1 have more frequency have number 2 etc. Calculate expected distribution using log formula by putting n = 1 to 9 and put it in % terms. Collect the data on the line items of  annual BS,P&L and CFS for the firm for the last 3-5 years or quarterly data Compute the Leading digit for all the line items for all the years Count the actual frequency of Digits 1-9 from the above analysis Compute the actual distribution of the digits Find the Cumulative difference by comparing the actual distribution with the expected distribution ( Ignore sign differences) Compute KS ( Kolmogorov Smirnov goodness of fit test) Value Find out  whether KS value is lower than the Cut-off value  : If yes, the firm is Non-Manipulative else the firm is Manipulative

Slide 530: EV/EBITDA multiple EV= MV of Equity + MV of Debt – Cash MV of equity = market cap, MV of debt = BV of debt which is equal to short term borrowing and long term borrowing, cash and cash equivalent is cash and current investments.  EV is applicable for both equity and debt investor. EBITDA- Earnings Before Interest Tax Depreciation and Amortization When we values many companies using price based multiple why most of the companies are overvalues: There are few types of company where DPS = EPS, DPS < EPS, DPS > EPS If earning per share is higher then dividend per share then DPR is lower and P/E depends on DPR hence the valuation is higher.  One solution to solve it is to go for modified DPR which is FCFE/PAT where FCFE = PAT+D&A +/ delta Capex +- Delta NWC +- Net debt Computed PAT removed non-recurring items & other incomes.
